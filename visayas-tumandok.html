History of the Tumandok

The Tumandok people, also known as the Suludnon or Panay-Bukidnon, are an indigenous group residing in the mountainous interior of Panay Island, Philippines, particularly in the provinces of Capiz, Antique, and Iloilo. Their name, "Tumandok," translates to "native to the place," reflecting their deep ancestral ties to the region.

The Tumandok developed a culture rich in oral traditions, including epic chants like the Sugidanon, intricate traditional dances such as the Binanog, and detailed embroidery known as panubok. Their traditional economy was primarily based on agriculture, utilizing shifting cultivation and, in some areas, permanent rice paddies.
