Indigenous People

The Philippines is home to numerous indigenous tribes, many of whom have preserved unique pre-colonial cultures and traditions. These groups are generally categorized as the "Igorot" in the Cordillera region, "Lumad" in Mindanao, and "Mangyan" on Mindoro. Other notable groups include the Aeta, Badjao, Palawano, and the Ifugao.

Culture Treasure

From the intricate weaves of the T'nalak cloth to the ancient chants of the Cordilleras, the cultural treasures of the Philippines represent the soul of its indigenous communities. These traditions, arts, and rituals are living testaments to the creativity, resilience, and wisdom passed down through generations.

Explore by Region

Luzon

Home to diverse indigenous groups like the Igorot and Aeta, known for rice terraces and mountain rituals.

Visayas

Known for Ati and Tumandok communities, preserving unique languages, dances, and traditions.

Mindanao

Rich with tribes like the Lumad and Manobo, showcasing vibrant textiles, music, and belief systems.

This website promotes awareness and appreciation for the Indigenous Peoples of the Philippines through accessible education and cultural highlights.
