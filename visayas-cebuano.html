History of the Cebuano

The history of the Cebuano people is rich and pivotal in the narrative of the Philippines. Cebu, being one of the earliest points of contact with European explorers, holds a significant place in the archipelago's past. Pre-colonial Cebu was a thriving trade center, known as Sugbu, with established relationships with neighboring islands and even traders from China and other parts of Southeast Asia. It was ruled by chieftains, with Rajah Humabon being the most notable during the arrival of Ferdinand Magellan in 1521.
