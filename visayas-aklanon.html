History of the Aklanon

Aklanon people, who primarily inhabit the province of Aklan in Panay Island, Philippines, is rich and predates Spanish colonization. Archaeological findings in Aklan, such as the Batan burial sites, suggest a long history of human settlement and a complex pre-colonial culture with trade links to other parts of Southeast Asia.

During the Spanish colonial era, Aklan was among the first areas to be evangelized. It was initially part of the larger province of Panay before eventually being established as a separate province. Aklanon history also includes significant participation in resistance movements against Spanish and later American rule.

The Aklanon people have a strong cultural identity, evident in their distinct Aklanon language, vibrant traditions like the Ati-Atihan festival (though primarily associated with Kalibo, the capital), and their unique customs and folklore shaped by their long history and connection to the land and sea. Today, Aklan continues to be a region proud of its heritage, balancing its historical roots with modern developments.
